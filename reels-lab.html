Six working prototypes of one section: the Mountain Glow regimen paired with Emily Doyle's short-form video. Same five products, same simulated reels, same routine state — only the UX changes. Scroll, tap, and add; the routine pill in the corner keeps score.

01

Prototype 01 · Inline feed

The Reel Rail

Meet shoppers in the grammar they arrived in — a thumb-able rail of five reels, one per routine step, each with its add button right underneath.

Why it worksZero learning curve — the exact grammar of the feed they came from. Watch outRails get skimmed; products 4–5 risk never being seen.
02

Prototype 02 · Immersive player

Story Mode

One tap, full immersion — the regimen as a four-minute story with an add moment built into the end of every chapter.

Why it worksStrongest narrative transportation — the regimen becomes one 4-minute ritual. Watch outIt's a modal; the entry card has to be irresistible or no one sees it.

Prototype 03 · Player + shelf

Split Stage

Keep the video and the shelf on one surface — selecting a product swaps the reel, so watching and adding never lose each other.

Why it worksWatching and adding are the same surface — no context switch to lose them in. Watch outHeaviest build; on mobile the player must not bury the product list.

Prototype 04 · Editorial still-life

The Founder's Shelf

Premium first — a still-life of the five vessels that hides the video inside the product. Touch one and it comes quietly to life.

Why it worksThe most premium treatment — video without the feed aesthetic. Watch outLowest video prominence; the affordance must be unmissable on touch.

Prototype 05 · The four-minute morning

Routine Timeline

Sell the routine as four minutes, not five products — a morning timeline where every stop carries a small reel and an add button.

Why it worksReframes five products as four minutes — attacks the "too much effort" objection head-on. Watch outTimeline metaphors drift decorative; keep it ruthlessly simple.
    Total: 4 minutes. The whole ritual takes less time than your coffee brews.
