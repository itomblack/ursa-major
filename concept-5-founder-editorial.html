The Glow Diaries · No. 04 · Vermont

A Founder's Essay · Skin After Forty

The serum I built when my skin stopped recognizing me.

Why I spent two winters formulating Mountain Glow — and the morning ritual that grew, one honest step at a time, around a single golden drop.

Words by Emily Doyle, co-founder, Ursa Major
Burlington, Vermont · An Ursa Major editorial

Chapter One — The Origin

I wasn't looking for a new product. I was missing my own face.

For most of my life I never thought much about my skin. Then I turned forty-six, and one ordinary Tuesday I caught my reflection in the bathroom mirror and didn't quite know the woman looking back. The lines weren't the problem — I've earned every one of them on trailheads and ski lifts. It was the flatness. The light had gone out. My skin had stopped doing the quiet, luminous thing it used to do on its own.

I run a skincare company. You'd think I'd have had an answer on the shelf. But everything formulated for "mature skin" felt like it was written by someone who had never been outside — heavy, perfumed, vaguely apologetic, as if aging were a condition to be managed rather than a life being lived. None of it spoke to a woman who still wanted to feel like herself at the top of a mountain in November.

I didn't want to look younger. I wanted to look like the light had come back on. Emily Doyle, on why she started

So I did the only thing I know how to do. I went back to the bench. Two Vermont winters, forty-one iterations, and a great deal of testing on my own forty-six-year-old face later, we had a single golden serum that did exactly what I'd been missing. We called it Mountain Glow, because that's the precise feeling — golden-hour light, on skin, at any hour.

The Serum, Up Close

What one golden drop is actually doing.

Mountain Glow is our anti-aging serum for mature skin — a lightweight amber treatment built around a stabilized retinal, a vitamin-C derivative, sea buckthorn, and a peptide blend. In plain terms, here is the work it quietly does, morning after morning.

  • Softens fine linesEncourages cell turnover so the first faint lines around the eyes and mouth blur and settle.
  • Eases deeper wrinklesSupports collagen over time, so the creases that came to stay look relaxed, not etched.
  • Restores firmnessThe peptide blend helps skin feel resilient and lifted, the way it did before gravity got opinions.
  • Deep hydrationSea buckthorn and humectants pull moisture into thirsty mature skin and hold it there all day.
  • Returns radianceThe vitamin-C derivative wakes up dull, flat tone — that is the glow the whole thing is named for.

An Honest Aside

The serum that started a ritual.

Here is the thing no one tells you. A great serum used alone is a great serum used alone. Mountain Glow on its own changed my skin — but the real transformation came when I stopped treating it like a magic bullet and started treating it like the centre of a small, deliberate ritual.

Skin that has lived a while wants support, not heroics. The cleanse that doesn't strip. The cream that seals the serum in. The eye care for the most delicate ground. The body, finally invited to the conversation. Each step makes Mountain Glow work harder and last longer — they are not extras, they are the rest of the sentence.

One drop is a moment. The ritual is what actually changes your skin. The bridge from product to practice

So what follows isn't a product list. It's my actual morning — the four steps I built, in order, around the serum that started it all. Read each one. If a step belongs in your morning too, add it to your routine in the panel that's following you down the page. Watch it fill, watch it save.

1 Step One — The Clean Slate

First, I wash away the night.Milky Cleanser

Before I reach for anything precious, I start with the gentlest possible reset. The Milky Cleanser turns from milk to the softest foam and lifts off sleep, sweat and the last of yesterday without ever leaving my skin tight or squeaky. Mature skin can't afford to be stripped — every bit of its own oil is doing a job. This step matters because the serum can only sink into skin that's clean and still intact.

2 Step Two — The Heart of It

Then, the golden drop everything turns on.Mountain Glow · the anchor

On clean skin, while it's still a little damp, three or four drops of Mountain Glow. I press it in — never rub — and wait the length of one slow breath while it disappears. This is the step that started the whole story, so it's already locked into your routine over in the panel; it's the base everyone else builds around. Everything before it prepares the skin to receive it. Everything after it protects the work it's doing.

3 Step Three — The Seal

After the serum, I reach for the cream.Alpine Rich Cream

A serum left on the surface is a serum half-wasted. The moment Mountain Glow has settled, I seal it in with Alpine Rich Cream — rich enough for January wind off Lake Champlain, but it melts in and never sits greasy. It's the lid on the jar, the all-day barrier that keeps every drop of serum working instead of evaporating. After fifty, this is the step I refuse to skip.

4 Step Four — The Delicate Ground

For the eyes, a lighter, smarter touch.Forest Alchemy Eye Cream

The skin around the eyes is the thinnest on the body and the first to tell on us. I tap on Forest Alchemy Eye Cream with my ring finger — the weakest one, so I can't tug. It brightens the shadow of a short night, de-puffs the morning, and softens the crow's-feet I get from squinting into alpine sun. It's the serum's specialist, doing precise work where the rich cream is simply too much.

5 Step Five — The Rest of You

And last, the body finally gets invited in.Body Butter

We pour everything into our faces and forget that skin doesn't stop at the jaw. The last thing I do is smooth on Body Butter — whipped, fast-absorbing, gone before I've buttoned my shirt. It's the step that makes the ritual feel complete rather than clinical, the same care extended from chin to ankle. Add this one and you've built the full five-step morning, exactly as I do it.

Last Word

Build the morning that fits your face.

There's no wrong place to begin. Some readers take just the serum and let it earn its place over a season. Others build the whole ritual in one go and never look back. Whatever you've gathered in your routine panel, it's there waiting — and the more of my morning you keep, the more we set aside for you.

With warmth from a cold state,
Emily
